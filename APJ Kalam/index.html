Tribute to A.P.J Abdul Kalam

About A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A.P.J Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a renowned aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the title.

Achievements

  • Developed India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Led India's successful nuclear tests in 1988
  • Wrote books like "Wings of Fire" and "India 2020"
  • Recieved the Bharat Ratna India's highest civilian award
